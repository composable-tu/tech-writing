# Distillation Notes

## Source

- **Article:** How to Write Technical Texts
- **URL:** https://azat.io/en/blog/how-to-write-technical-texts
- **Author:** Azat S.

## Goal

Distill the **writing methodology** proposed by the article, not the author's personal style or voice.

## What Was Distilled

The article is a prescriptive guide. It proposes specific rules for:

1. How to construct sentences and choose grammatical forms.
2. How to structure paragraphs, sections, introductions, and conclusions.
3. How to edit for accessibility, brevity, objectivity, and clarity.
4. How to write and present code examples in technical texts.
5. How to maintain relevance and correctness over time.

## What Was Intentionally Excluded

- The author's personal tone or stylistic habits.
- Specific examples about JavaScript, TypeScript, React, Vue, GraphQL, etc., except where they illustrate a general rule.
- The exact Before/After examples, except as minimal illustrations in the SKILL.md.

## Design Decisions

- The SKILL.md uses Chinese because the user asked in Chinese.
- The source article is in English, so the knowledge base preserves both the original structure and the distilled methodology in English for traceability.
- A checklist is provided as a practical tool for applying the methodology.
